Team S2 Formed — Five Members, Two Continents

All five positions in Team S2 are now filled. One member from UTAD in Portugal, four from Jilin University in China — together responsible for the data pipeline that the whole DSD2026 system runs on.

Team S2 has been constituted as part of the DSD 2025–2026 programme, the collaborative distributed software development course running between the Universidade de Trás-os-Montes e Alto Douro (UTAD) in Portugal and Jilin University (JLU) in China.

The Team

Five members have been confirmed. Zhiqi Zhang has been designated as Project Manager; specific role assignments for the remaining four members will follow shortly:

  • Zhiqi Zhang (CN · JLU) — Project Manager
  • João Neves (PT · UTAD)
  • Haobo Lin (CN · JLU)
  • Pengxu Chen (CN · JLU)
  • Tianze Wang (CN · JLU)

S2's Place in the Project

Team S2 belongs to the Sensor layer, alongside Team S1. While S1 handles sensor firmware and the wireless communication protocol, S2 is responsible for the data acquisition pipeline that receives structured data from S1 and delivers it to the Server layer.

S2's most critical early responsibility is the mock sensor data module — a mandatory Sprint 1 deliverable. This module generates realistic, schema-compliant data without requiring physical hardware, which immediately unblocks four other teams (V1, V2, M1, M2) to begin their own development in parallel.

Working Across Timezones

Portugal and China operate roughly 7–8 hours apart depending on daylight saving. S2 has adopted an async-first workflow to handle this gap: João Neves as Vice PM leads the Portuguese side during the PM's off-hours, and posts a daily standup update to the shared GitHub Wiki at the end of each PT working day. Real-time sync meetings are handled via VooV (WeMeet) on an agreed rotating schedule.
